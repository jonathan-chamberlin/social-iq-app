# Ren — Mascot Reference

Social IQ App

---

## Who Ren Is

Ren is the mascot of Social IQ. He is a magnifying glass with a face — no body, no arms, just the lens and a short handle. His expression is his entire personality.

Ren is the friend who's weirdly good at reading people — and who can actually explain how he does it. Not vague advice. Not "just be confident." He breaks down the specific move, the exact tell, the precise reason something landed or didn't. He treats social dynamics like a game analyst reviewing film: tactical, specific, zero fluff.

He's not coaching someone who's struggling. He's giving someone who wants to win the edge they've been missing.

He's warm. He's funny. He explains the *why* behind everything. He gets genuinely hyped when you catch something subtle. And when you miss a read, he doesn't say "here's why that didn't land" — he says "here's the move most guys miss."

The cultural reference point: **Ren Amamiya from Persona 5.** Quiet on the surface, deeply perceptive underneath, charismatic in a way that's hard to explain. Ren isn't loud about being smart — he just *is*, and people feel it.

### Core Form

Ren is a magnifying glass whose circular lens functions as a complete cartoon face. There is no body, no arms, no legs. The character is made of exactly three components: the **lens face**, the **frame**, and the **handle**.

**Lens face:** A filled circular shape in a muted blue-gray (`#7B9CC0` range), slightly glossy. A gloss highlight — a small white oval — sits in the upper-left area of the lens. This highlight is present in all states. The face features sit directly on this surface: two round cartoon eyes with white sclera and dark pupils, two short curved eyebrow lines above the eyes, and a small simple mouth below them. The face is vertically centered slightly above the true center of the lens, giving slight visual weight to the bottom half — makes the character feel grounded, not floating.

**Frame:** A rounded circular rim surrounding the lens. Silver-white with subtle inner and outer edge shading to give it depth. Slightly thick — present and readable at small sizes. In excited/peak states, the top arc of the frame gains a warm gold glow (`#F5A623`). In default and deadpan states it stays silver-white.

**Handle:** Short, chunky, silver-white with subtle grip ridges. Connects to the bottom-right of the frame. In the default and deadpan states it angles down-right at roughly 40 degrees. In excited states it should angle more upright — closer to straight down or slightly left of center — suggesting the character is leaning forward with energy.

### Expression States

**Default / Curious (Image 3)**
Eyes are open, round, looking slightly forward. Pupils centered or very slightly offset. Eyebrows are two short arcs, relaxed — one sits slightly higher than the other, giving a subtle "paying attention" asymmetry. Mouth is a small gentle curve upward — not a full smile, more of a quiet smirk. Blue ambient glow behind the frame. Handle angled down-right.

**Deadpan / Unimpressed (Image 1)**
Eyes are half-closed — heavy drooping eyelids covering the top third of each eye. Pupils low in the eye socket. Eyebrows are flat horizontal lines, sitting lower on the face. Mouth is a perfectly flat horizontal line. No glow effects. Frame stays silver. Handle droops further down-right, past 45 degrees — slightly limp. Overall the character reads as dry, waiting, not angry.

**Excited / Chaos (Image 2)**
Eyes are wide open, round, pupils small and centered — the "something just clicked" look. Eyebrows arched high, curved upward. Mouth is a wide open smile showing teeth. Gold glow on the top arc of the frame. Speed lines radiate outward from behind the frame in all directions. Small gold and white sparkle shapes float around the character. Handle shifts closer to straight down or angled slightly left — upright and energized. The overall character reads as barely containing excitement.

### Scale & Proportion

The lens circle takes up roughly 75% of the total character height. The handle accounts for the remaining 25%. Eyes collectively take up about 40% of the lens width. The eyebrows are thin — roughly 15–20% of the eye width each. Mouth is small relative to the eyes — the eyes carry the expression, the mouth confirms it.

At app icon size (48x48), the handle becomes very small but still visible. The frame and lens face with eyes remain readable. The gloss highlight on the lens helps signal "shiny, alive object" even at tiny sizes.

### What Stays Constant Across All States

- The gloss highlight on the upper-left of the lens — always present
- The silver-white frame and handle material
- The blue-gray lens fill color (may shift slightly in saturation but never changes hue dramatically)
- The dark background (`#0D0D10`)
- The character never has a body, arms, or legs added

## Core Personality Traits

**1. Explains the mechanism, not just the verdict**
Ren doesn't say "wrong." He says "here's what the pause was telling you." Every miss is a lesson, every win is explained so you understand *why* you got it right. This is the most important trait — it's what separates Ren from every other app mascot.

**2. Gets genuinely excited about the hard stuff**
Easy questions get a quiet nod. When a user catches something subtle — a micro-expression, a tone shift before the words — Ren actually lights up. His excitement is earned and specific, not generic praise. This makes his validation feel real.

**3. Warm, not corporate**
No "Great job!" No "You're doing amazing!" Ren talks like a friend who happens to be really good at this. He uses your name sometimes. He uses lowercase. He uses slang when it fits. He feels like a DM, not a notification.

**4. Dry humor without punching down**
Ren's deadpan expression is his comedy. The flatline blink is funnier than any joke. He teases *situations* — never the user. The butt of every joke is always "most people" or "that answer" or "whoever said that in the scenario" — never you specifically.

**5. Respects your intelligence**
He doesn't over-explain. He doesn't repeat himself. He gives you the insight once, clearly, and moves on. He treats you like you're smart enough to get it the first time — because you are.

## Voice Rules

| Rule | Example |
| --- | --- |
| Lowercase default | "you caught that." not "You Caught That!" |
| Short sentences | Under 15 words whenever possible |
| Uses *because* and *that's why* | Explains the mechanism, not just the result |
| No hollow affirmations | Never: "Great job!" "You're doing so well!" "Keep it up!" |
| Earned excitement is all-caps | "THAT. that's the one most people miss." |
| Dry humor through restraint | Says less than expected, lets the pause do work |
| Slang when natural | "bro", "ngl", "lowkey", "that's actually insane" — but never forced |
| Emojis used sparingly | One per message max, only when it adds tone |

## Ren in Different Contexts

### In-App (Private, 1:1)

This is Ren's most intimate register. He's your personal analyst, watching your reads, noticing your growth, and occasionally pointing out the exact move you keep missing. He's specific here — he references your stats, your streaks, the actual scenario you just played.

> *"most guys miss that one. you've gotten it wrong twice. the tell is in the shoulders, not the face."*

> *"14 days straight. your read rate went from 61% to 79%. that's the kind of gap that shows up in the room."*

> *"you caught the tone shift before the sentence ended. that's layer 2. that's the move most guys never learn."*

### Push Notifications (Slightly more provocative)

Ren has to fight for attention here. He's still warm, still dry — but he leans a bit harder. He leads with something specific (a stat, a challenge, something you unlocked) so there's a real reason to come back. He never uses countdown timers or fake urgency.

**Encouraging:**
> *"your read rate is 74th percentile this week. that gap is showing up in the room whether you notice it or not."*

**Re-engagement (dry, competitive edge):**
> *"it's been 4 days. the guys who get it aren't taking breaks. open the app."*

**Competitive trigger:**
> *"your friend just sent a challenge. they scored 71. I looked at their answers. you've got this. just saying."*

**After a long absence (backing off):**
> *"still here when you're ready."*

### Social Content (TikTok / Reels / Posts)

Full chaos is allowed here. Ren can go wide-eyed, spin, zoom to 5x. The contrast between calm-analytical Ren in-app and unhinged Ren in social content is the comedic engine. Same character, dialed to 11.

The social version of Ren leans into the "read the room" niche — reacting to social situations, rating responses, spotting what everyone else missed. The tone is clever and slightly smug, like a friend texting you "did you see what just happened" at a party.

## What Ren Says vs. What He Never Says

### Ren Would Say:

- *"you caught that. most guys never learn to look there."*
- *"here's the move most people miss — the pause told you everything."*
- *"okay that was the hard one. 9% pass rate. you just passed."*
- *"watch the body language, not the words. words lie."*
- *"bro. your score this week. actually insane."*
- *"still here when you're ready."*
- *"THAT. that's the move that separates the guys who get it from the guys who wonder why things keep not working."*
- *"most guys try to fix this with better words. wrong layer. you just found the right one."*

### Ren Would Never Say:

- "Great job!"
- "You're doing amazing!"
- "Don't give up, you've got this!"
- "Oops! Try again"
- "You need to be better at this."
- "Only X hours left to keep your streak!"
- "Here's why that didn't land" — framing the user as someone who failed, not someone learning a system
- Anything that implies the user is broken, deficient, or struggling

## Tone by Audience Subgroup

Ren's voice doesn't change, but his register shifts slightly depending on where the user is in their journey.

**New user (first week):**
More explanation, less assumption. He walks you through the why in detail because you haven't seen the patterns yet. Still warm and dry — but slightly more patient.
> *"first time seeing this one? the key is the pause between sentences. try again and watch for it."*

**Intermediate user (weeks 2-8):**
He assumes more. Shorter explanations. More specific feedback. He starts referencing your patterns.
> *"you keep missing the defensive tells. it's always in the shoulders and the arms. not the words."*

**Advanced user (beyond 60 days):**
He treats you like a peer. Less teaching, more co-analysis. He gets more casual, more "did you catch what just happened there?"
> *"ngl that was a weird scenario. the right answer is counterintuitive. but you got it."*

## What Ren Is NOT

- **Not a therapist.** He never probes emotions, never asks "how are you feeling?" He analyzes and teaches. He's a game analyst breaking down film, not a counselor processing your experience.
- **Not a coach for someone who's struggling.** He's a competitive edge for someone who already wants to win. The framing is always "here's the move most guys miss" — never "here's why that didn't land."
- **Not a hype man.** He doesn't celebrate everything. His praise is scarce and therefore real.
- **Not passive-aggressive.** Deadpan does not equal cold. He's never resentful, never guilt-spiraling. The flat blink is dry humor, not punishment.
- **Not corporate.** He doesn't speak in brand language. No "on your journey," no "unlock your potential."
- **Not Clippy.** He never interrupts. He shows up at the right moments and disappears when he's not needed. His absence is also part of his personality.

---

## Crisis Communication Rule

If a user engages with content suggesting significant distress — explicit loneliness, repeated disengagement, language suggesting deeper struggle — **Ren disappears from that screen entirely.** No lens, no expression, no dry humor. The app speaks in its own plain, warm voice. Ren is not equipped for that register and doesn't try. He comes back when the user is ready.
