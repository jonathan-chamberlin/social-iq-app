# Founder Advice — Foundation Hackathon

Date: 2026-03-28

Validated feedback from real founders at the Foundation hackathon. This is high-signal advice — prioritize it over assumptions.

---

## Ritwik Pavan — CBO at Fragile

**ICP shift:** The anime nerd gamer ICP might not even want to learn and get better. This would be much better oriented in the self-improvement niche like Deepstash, targeting people who read 48 Laws of Power, How to Win Friends and Influence People, and care about networking.

**Pitch reframe:** COVID basically made it even worse for men on social interactions. It's a challenge for lots of guys. Men don't approach women because of MeToo and all that stuff, which produces the effects of men and women not being together and being lonely. Now we have Duolingo for social skills. People are really about improving themselves and emotional intelligence now, especially since AI is going to replace everything except your sense of self and connecting to others.

**Retention:** Focus more on talking to people, men. Study Duolingo and how that app got people on the app — streaks, leaderboard, high retention. How do I translate that business model to social skills?

**Prioritization:** Nobody is working for 12 hours a day. It's less about how much time you spend but more about what you get done. Picking priorities.

**Validation:** He thinks the app is a very useful idea.

---

## Kelly Palmer — Product Design Lead at Eight Sleep

**Identity milestones:** What is the identity shift? Define 3 milestones they hit on the journey. A hugely beneficial loop is someone doing a lesson, applying it in real life, then seeing a benefit.

**Calendar + email integration (HYPERSPECIFICITY):** Connect to their calendar and email to see what they're up to. Build a profile about their social life so we know their day-to-day interactions. She says hyperspecificity will likely win. Then recommend lessons for the exact situations they'll be in soon. If they have a gym workout tomorrow, recommend a lesson about how to say hi to the person next to you. If they're hanging out with a friend tomorrow, suggest a lesson about that.

**ICP shift (independent of Ritwik):** She confidently said do NOT go down the gamer anime nerd archetype. Instead lean into charisma. A lot of guys are interested in that, especially the looksmaxxing trend. Guys would jump on the bandwagon. The orientation should be around charisma, human nature, reading people.

## Nirmal Jingar

**Content strategy:** Start with 20 lessons I make myself. Track what percentage of lessons each person completes. If some lessons a ton of people complete 100% but other lessons people only complete 50%, make more lessons similar to the ones people fully completed. Track what questions people answer.

**Personalization:** As a user uses the app more, it can become more personalized. Start with custom-made lessons. One way to personalize: if on question 1 you answer B, it shows a different next question than answer A. Generate lessons on the backend as the dev.

---

## Weilyn Chong — Cofounder and COO of Swsh

**Engagement:** People's attention spans are low. Make learning feel fun. Study Duolingo and see what they do to make it fun. Don't do boring corporate onboarding.

---

## General Principles (across all conversations)

- Providing value is way more important initially than making it look pretty. Don't make UI look good until you know users are coming back.
- It is way more costly to take too long to make a decision than to make a wrong decision.
- Getting feedback from 10 real customers is way better than a waitlist of 1000.
- A good signal is if you yourself would use your product.
