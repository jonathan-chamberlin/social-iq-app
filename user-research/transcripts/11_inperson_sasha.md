## Raw Notes
Sasha is not the ICP. He's socially confident with a binary approach — engage or don't — and doesn't feel a need to improve. His feedback is valuable as a non-ICP perspective: the product reads as something for people with real social anxiety, not for ambitious people who already feel capable. He sees the audience as people with genuine social fear, not upwardly-mobile self-improvement guys. Suggested "more levels of complexity" — more content depth. Said to "figure out what people are really worried about" — whether it's talking to others, self-image issues, etc. Couldn't identify specific good/bad product elements due to lack of experience with this type of product.

## Top 3 Takeaways
1. Non-ICP user confirms the product doesn't resonate with socially confident people — validates targeting self-improvement males who actively want charisma skills, not those who already feel "good"
2. Hard-coded quiz with single output was immediately noticed and questioned — dynamic/personalized pathways are table stakes
3. Product reads to confident users as targeting people with social anxiety — need to reframe positioning away from "fix your social anxiety" toward "level up your influence"
