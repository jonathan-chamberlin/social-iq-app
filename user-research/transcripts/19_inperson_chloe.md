## What Happened

Handed her the phone mid-conversation. She did not go through onboarding — dropped straight into a lesson. Answered 2 questions, then clicked out. Did not complete a full lesson.

## Feedback

**Colors too dark / heavy.**
- Wants lighter colors. Current black-background + gold palette read as too intense / not inviting to her. She designs a consumer iOS app herself (Summet) — this is a taste signal from someone in the target aesthetic audience, not a random preference.

**Lessons should feel like a conversation, not a single point-in-time decision.**
- Current format: read a scenario → pick an answer → move on. She wants it to continue — the scenario should keep unfolding based on her answer. Back-and-forth, not one-shot multiple choice.
- This is the branching-scenario pattern several male users also hinted at (see Aryaman, Nishanth, Brandon, Kyle, Wyatt, Avi in the feedback matrix). Chloe articulated it most explicitly: it should feel like a conversation, not a test question.

**"You're saying there's a right answer but for some of these situations there isn't one right answer."**
- Direct challenge to the single-correct-answer premise for social scenarios. Maps to synthesis Pattern 3 (answer authority, 8/20) and specifically the epistemological variant raised by Elijah and Avi ("can you even know what's right in social situations?"). Chloe is the first ICP-female to raise it explicitly.
- Ties back to her "conversation not a point in time" framing — if it were a conversation that unfolded based on her answer, there wouldn't need to be one "right" answer; there'd be consequences that play out from whatever she chose.

**Clicked out after 2 questions.**
- Skipped onboarding (not her fault — handed mid-session) so no investment / no framing for why she's doing it.
- No hooked pain point on screen, no progress signal, so nothing pulled her through to lesson 3+.

---

## Key Signals

1. **Lighter color palette request** — first explicit ask from an ICP-female designer. Worth weighing heavily given her taste credential (consumer iOS app designer in the same audience).
2. **"Conversation not a point in time" + "not one right answer"** — these are the same critique from two angles. She's saying the format is wrong AND the premise (one correct answer) is wrong. The branching/conversational format is the structural fix for both: if the scenario plays out based on her choice, there's no single "right answer" — only consequences. Strongest articulation yet of this thread; joins 10+ users already on it per synthesis Patterns 1, 9, 12.
3. **No onboarding = no completion** — reinforces that the onboarding flow is doing real work (pain-point priming, investment, framing). Dropping a user in cold = immediate bounce. Don't let testers skip it in future sessions.
