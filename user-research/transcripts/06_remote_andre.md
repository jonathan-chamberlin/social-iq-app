## Raw Notes
Andre's feedback is high-signal because he's perfect ICP and articulate. Two big themes: (1) the experience should feel personalized/individual, not one-size-fits-all — he referenced Apple's design philosophy of personal expression; (2) the wrong-answer feedback should come at the end, not mid-flow, to feel less judgmental and more like a conversation. He also acknowledged that truly adaptive content (AI reading open text input) is harder but would be the ideal version. His "no judgment" comparison to ChatGPT is worth noting — the app should feel like a safe space to get things wrong, not a test.

## Top 3 Takeaways
1. **Personalization is the aspiration** — app should adapt to the person (Apple philosophy), not force everyone through same path
2. **Defer wrong-answer feedback to end** — mid-flow correction feels judgmental; end-of-lesson review feels like growth (3rd person to say this)
3. **15-minute actionable sessions validated** — "flows perfectly" for the time commitment
