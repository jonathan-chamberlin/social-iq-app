## Reaction

## UX Issues
- **READ/THINK/SPEAK indicators look tappable.** Tamok clicked on the step indicators (READ, THINK, SPEAK capsules) at the top of a lesson expecting them to navigate between steps. They're not interactive — just labels for the current step. This is a UX affordance problem: the capsule styling suggests they're tabs.

## Feedback
- **"How do you know that these are the best things to say?"** — Same credibility/authority question raised by Phoebe, Lauren, Kofi, Nishanth, and Elijah in previous sessions. Tamok asked this despite the explanations being present on each answer card. This reinforces the pattern: users either skip the explanations or don't find them sufficient as credibility signals.

## Jonathan's Notes
- Multiple users have now asked "how do you know" — the explanations alone aren't enough. Need an explicit credibility signal.
- CharmXP (competitor) claims their model is "supported by Cornell and UC Berkeley research" on their paywall. Could add similar research-backed framing to Social IQ — either on the paywall, in the onboarding, or within lesson explanations themselves.

## Top Takeaways
1. **Step indicators need de-affordance** — either make them non-interactive visually (no capsule fill, just text) or make them actually tappable for navigation
2. **Credibility signal is now a recurring pattern** — 6+ users across both ICP segments have questioned answer authority. Need to surface "why this is right" more prominently, possibly with research citations or a credibility badge
