# Sorority Feedback — DEEF

**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Source:** Lauren, Sana, and Phoebe Shore — sorority DEEF

## Reaction

When told the app is "like Duolingo but it teaches social skills," all three lit up and said: "We need that app because girls in our sorority don't know how to talk to guys."

## Example They Gave

At a formal, a girl approached a guy and said "I know you." He responded "Where?" — suspicious. She said "From the sorority wedding," referencing an event from a year ago. He was weirded out.

## Additional Feedback

**Approaching guys:** Girls need help approaching guys — badly. This was a strong consensus point, not just one person's opinion.

**Taking compliments:** One girl pointed out that girls don't know how to take a compliment. Example: when she says "omg I love your top," girls respond one of two ways:

1. **Deflecting:** "No omg it's nothing" — dismisses the compliment entirely
2. **Reflexive returning:** "Aw thanks I love yours too" — feels insincere because if you actually loved it, why didn't you say it first? It reads as obligatory, not genuine.

## Takeaways

- The pain point is real and recognized within the sorority — it's not just individual, it's cultural/group-level awareness
- The specific gap: knowing how to open a conversation without coming across as awkward or overly familiar
- The example illustrates poor calibration — referencing an obscure event from a year ago as an opener signals low social awareness to the other person
- Approaching guys is a major pain point — potential lesson topic with high pull
- Compliment responses are a subtle but common social skill gap — both deflecting and reflexive returning signal insecurity or insincerity
- These are two distinct lesson-worthy scenarios: initiating conversation and receiving praise gracefully
