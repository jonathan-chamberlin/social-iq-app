## Raw Notes
Brandon engaged thoughtfully but kept surfacing the nuance problem — social situations don't have one "right" answer. He specifically noted that professional dynamics vary by manager personality, not just situation. He was confused about whether the scenario was one continuous interaction or multiple interactions — Jonathan noted this means the Read/Think/Speak stages should be one continuous screen, not three separate pages. Brandon said 4 answer options is the right number ("any more is too many, any less is not good"). His key philosophical point: "You can't teach nuance without showing the right answer. Something that's objectively right." This validates the MCQ approach but acknowledges its limitation.

## Top 3 Takeaways
1. **Scenario context must be crystal clear** — is this a group setting or 1:1? One continuous interaction or separate? Ambiguity kills immersion
2. **Read/Think/Speak should be one continuous screen** — splitting across pages breaks the flow and causes confusion
3. **Nuance is real but you still need a "right" answer** — "you can't teach nuance without showing the right answer"
