# Founder Advice — Foundation Hacks (Harvard, March 2026)

## Highest-Signal Takeaways (Multiple Founders Converged)

### 1. ICP is wrong — shift from introvert/gamer to self-improvement/charisma
- **Ritwik (CBO, Fragile):** The anime nerd gamer might not even want to improve. Better fit: self-improvement niche. Guys who read 48 Laws of Power, How to Win Friends. People who care about networking and emotional intelligence.
- **Kelly Palmer (Product Design Lead, Eight Sleep):** Don't go down the gamer archetype. Lean into charisma and reading people. The looksmaxxing trend proves guys want to level up — Social IQ is the social side of that.
- **Signal strength: HIGH.** Two unrelated founders said the same thing unprompted.

### 2. Value first, polish later
- **Ritwik, Weilyn, Kelly (all independently):** Don't make UI look good until you know users are coming back. Providing value matters more than looking pretty.
- **Weilyn (COO, Swsh):** They spent a lot of time making one app's UI great — nobody used it. Made another app with terrible UI — people kept coming back because it provided value.

### 3. Start with hand-made content, track completion, personalize over time
- **Nirmal Jingar:** Start with 20 lessons you make yourself. Track what percentage of lessons each person completes. If some lessons get 100% completion and others get 50%, make more like the 100% ones. Track what questions people answer — use that to personalize over time.
- **Nirmal:** Generate lessons on the backend as the dev. Don't have AI generate them client-side.

### 4. Hyperspecificity wins
- **Kelly Palmer:** Connect to their calendar and email. Build a profile of their social life. Recommend lessons for the exact situations they'll face soon — gym workout tomorrow, friend hangout, work meeting. The more specific to their actual life, the more it sticks.
- **Note:** This is a V2+ feature, not V1. But it tells you where the product goes.

### 5. Retention mechanics — study Duolingo
- **Weilyn:** People's attention spans are low. Make learning feel fun. Study Duolingo specifically — streaks, leaderboards, XP. Figure out how to translate that business model to social skills.

### 6. Identity shift + real-world loop
- **Kelly Palmer:** What are the 3 identity milestones a user hits on their journey? The most powerful loop: do a lesson → apply it in real life → see a benefit → come back for more. Design for that loop.

## Tactical Advice

### Pitching / Framing
- **Ritwik's broader pitch:** COVID made social interaction worse for men. Men don't approach women because of cultural shifts. Loneliness epidemic. AI replaces everything except human connection. Now we have Duolingo for social skills. People care about EI and self-improvement more than ever.

### Decision-Making
- **Ritwik / panel:** It's way more costly to take too long to make a decision than to make a wrong decision.
- **Panel:** Getting feedback from 10 real customers is way better than a waitlist of 1000.
- **Panel:** A good signal is if you yourself would use your product.

### Productivity
- **Ritwik:** Nobody is working 12 hours a day. It's less about how much time you spend and more about what you get done. Pick priorities.
