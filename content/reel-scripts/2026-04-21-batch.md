## 1. The frat president's body language

**Source:** [Notion — Griffin gift scene (Apr 20)](https://www.notion.so/348afe0dcf03809982b2d758da83f161)

### SCRIPT

"Just studying man."

That's what the fraternity president said to me with his hands in his pockets and his shoulders slightly rounded.

I'd gone to his place to pick up a gift, a book and a pin. While I was there I figured I should make small talk. His roommate Nathan mentioned his co-op ends in two months, so I asked "what are your plans with that?"

But neither of them was replying with much energy. That's when I asked "what are you guys up to the rest of the day?"

Griffin did this slightly cringe smile, hands in his pockets, shoulders a bit rounded, and said "just studying man."

That was the answer I needed. His body language was telling me he wanted to get back to what he was doing. So I said "well, thank you for the gift," stepped forward to shake his hand, and left.

He looked slightly relieved.

I used to miss these cues. I thought leaving quickly felt rude, so I'd keep talking. Now I read the hands and the shoulders, and I leave when the body is already gone.

**just studying man**

- how to know when to leave someone's apartment
- I used to miss this body language cue)

## 2. I refused to blame his childhood

**Source:** [Notion — Hudson anxiety call (Apr 21)](https://www.notion.so/349afe0dcf03802c9e01f61688995924)

### SCRIPT

"But they don't do anything! You're hyping me up man."

My friend Hudson called me feeling down. He said he had a lot of anxiety about stuff.

At one point he said "yeah man, I guess you helped me realize that I'm getting these feelings because I used to feel powerless in childhood."

I said "maybe. I don't really like speculating about what childhood stuff caused the current situation."

So I kept asking questions. The one that changed things was "do you have any ideas about things you could do differently to make this anxiety come up less often?"

He listed them out: good sleep, working out, something for his career, and something fun like piano. On those days, the anxiety is way less.

He already had the answer. He just needed someone to keep asking instead of reaching for a childhood trauma explanation.

Then I did the identity labeling thing. I said "there's two types of people. There's the people who say 'I kinda know what to do buuuut…'"

Hudson interrupted me. "But they don't do anything! You're hyping me up man."

He named himself into the doer group. That was the whole trick. The question did the work, and the label sealed it.

**I refused to blame his childhood**

- the question that fixed my friend's anxiety)

## 3. The rude lady at the blood test

**Source:** [Notion — Quest Diagnostics worker (Apr 6)](https://www.notion.so/33aafe0dcf0380629cb9deef7165d695)

### SCRIPT

"No you're not."

The woman at Quest Diagnostics said this to me after I told her I was there for a blood test. She paused for a few seconds before actually explaining what she meant.

I tried to help. I said "I have the script, maybe you can scan the barcode on it?"

She said "I know how it works my friend," with a tone.

I had two options. I could match the energy and make this worse, or I could try to figure out why she was being like this. I tried the second one.

I asked "busy day so far?"

She said "yeah."

"That must get stressful sometimes."

And then she said "yeah, especially since this is giving me more work at the end of my shift."

That was rude. But it was also the truth. Her meanness was about the work piling up at the end of her shift. I was just standing where the pile landed.

Asking about her day pulled that out in fifteen seconds. I still think it was rude to say out loud. But I'm glad I asked. I left knowing the mean voice came from her shift.

### ON-SCREEN TITLE

**no you're not**

(other options:
- I know how it works my friend
- the Quest Diagnostics worker was rude on purpose
- how I got a rude stranger to explain herself
- I almost matched her energy)

#storytime #relatable #dayinmylife

### TIKTOK CAPTION

#storytime #relatable #life

## 4. I used to crush my friends' ideas

**Source:** [Notion — Nick's 12 page plan (Apr 16)](https://www.notion.so/344afe0dcf0380749803ed129cc14e23)

### SCRIPT

I used to crush my friends' ideas.

My friend Nick made a 12-page business plan. He hadn't talked to a single person about it, done any online research, or built a mockup.

A younger version of me would have said "bro, you're skipping the most important part." And he would have deflated.

So I tried something else. I said "I'm so glad you actually took action and made a business plan. Most people keep it as an idea and it sits there forever. I'm sure that wasn't easy."

He agreed enthusiastically.

Then I kept going. "You're the type of person that takes action. So it's really important for where you're at to validate that people actually want this. Check on social media, check subreddits, see if people have the problem you're solving. How do you think that would be helpful?"

I ended on a question. It made him figure out the next step himself, which meant he got the credit for the idea. He walked away feeling like a person who validates before building.

### ON-SCREEN TITLE

**I used to crush my friends' ideas**

(other options:
- I'm so glad you took action
- you're the type of person that takes action
- my friend had a 12 page business plan and zero customers
- how to tell a friend their plan is incomplete)

### INSTAGRAM CAPTION

he made a 12 page business plan. he hadn't talked to a single customer. I didn't want to crush him.

#storytime #relatable #friendship

### TIKTOK CAPTION

#storytime #relatable #friendship

### HAND LANDMARKS

## 5. How is your skin so clear?

**Source:** [Notion — Gym strangers compliments (Mar 12)](https://www.notion.so/321afe0dcf0380ffa43bdcb719806f31)

### SCRIPT

"Bro, how is your skin so clear?"

I was at the gym next to a guy I'd never talked to. He took his earbuds out. I asked him that.

He smiled. Then he explained his routine: what he ate, how often he washed his face.

A couple of weeks later, different guy, same setup. I asked "how are your shoulders so big?"

He smiled too. He told me what he was doing.

I used to think complimenting a stranger was weird. I thought they'd be suspicious, or that I'd be wasting their time. But both times the response was the same. A small smile, and then they wanted to tell me.

There's usually something a person has been working hardest on, and nobody has asked about it in weeks. When you do, you hand them recognition for it. The more specific the question, the bigger the smile.

### ON-SCREEN TITLE

**how is your skin so clear?**

(other options:
- bro, how are your shoulders so big?
- I talked to two strangers at the gym on purpose
- the question that makes strangers light up
- I used to think this was weird)

### INSTAGRAM CAPTION

I asked a stranger at the gym the question he'd been waiting for someone to ask.

#storytime #gym #relatable

### TIKTOK CAPTION

I asked a stranger at the gym how his skin was so clear and he smiled

#storytime #gym #dayinmylife

### HAND LANDMARKS

1. skin so clear (opener quote)
2. earbuds out (moment of asking)
3. smiled then explained (his reaction)
4. shoulders so big (second experiment)
5. specificity (the lesson)

(say these exact: "how is your skin so clear?" and "how are your shoulders so big?")
