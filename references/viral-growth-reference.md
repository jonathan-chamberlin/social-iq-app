# Viral Growth Reference

Social IQ App

---

## K-Factor Mechanic — Challenge Share

This is the primary viral mechanic. Modeled on Impulse Brain Training's challenge share.

**Flow:**

1. User completes a scenario → gets score + percentile
2. App says: *"You read that situation better than 81% of people. Think your friend can?"*
3. One tap generates a shareable message: *"I just got 94/100 on this EQ test — I bet you can't beat me [link]"*
4. Recipient lands on that exact scenario, plays it, gets their own score
5. Prompted to compare scores → must sign up to see full results

**Why it works:**
- Sharer looks smart, competitive, and self-aware — not socially deficient
- Recipient has immediate motivation (beat the score)
- Conversion happens through FOMO + investment (they already played before hitting the paywall)

**Target K-factor:** 1.3–1.5 if scenarios are engaging enough to replay

**Key principle:** The share never signals "I have a problem." It signals "I'm good at something — prove you are too."

## Viral Growth Loop

```
User completes scenario
        ↓
Gets score + identity label ("you're a natural empath")
        ↓
Challenged to share ("can your friend beat this?")
        ↓
Friend plays → sees score → signs up to track progress
        ↓
Friend completes scenarios → shares → loop repeats
        ↓
Churned users receive: "Your friend just beat your score" push notification
        ↑___________________________________________________|
```

The re-engagement hook is what makes this self-sustaining. Duolingo's most powerful retention tool is the "you lost your streak" shame notification. Ours is social comparison: someone you know just outperformed you.

**The one thing that kills all of this: bad scenarios.** The scenario is the product. Everything else — the viral loop, the share mechanic, the onboarding — is distribution scaffolding around content quality.

## Short Onboarding for Challenge Link Flow

When a friend sends someone a challenge link, the recipient is already sold. Friction should be near zero:
- Play the scenario first
- Require sign-up to see the full score comparison
- No long questionnaire — they convert through investment in the game itself
