## Raw Notes
Nishanth was intellectually engaged but not personally invested. He didn't read the explanations — a critical signal. His main argument was that answers differ by industry/context (banking culture is different from tech), and being told you're wrong mid-lesson erodes trust in the app's authority. He wanted a "pick all answers, get feedback at end" flow. However, his not reading explanations suggests the real issue is text density — this is another data point for "less text, more visuals." His industry-context objection has some validity for workplace scenarios but less so for dating/social scenarios. He seemed disengaged overall — likely not ICP despite technically doing L2.

## Top 3 Takeaways
1. **He didn't read explanations** — strongest signal yet that text walls don't work, even when the content is good
2. **Mid-lesson "wrong" feedback may erode trust** — consider deferred feedback model (answer all, review at end) as an alternative
3. **Workplace scenarios need industry context** — what's "correct" in tech culture vs. finance culture differs meaningfully
