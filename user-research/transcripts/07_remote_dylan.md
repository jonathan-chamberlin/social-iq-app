## Raw Notes
Dylan's feedback is blunt and high-signal. He has product sense from building Chrome extensions and immediately identified the two missing layers: (1) structural direction — no clear path, users don't know what to do next or why; (2) soul — no mascot, no sound effects, no personality, no character that makes you want to come back. His "typical Base44 website" comment is a gut punch but accurate — the prototype looks like a prototype, not a product. He said "No" to using it again, which is the clearest negative signal in the dataset. He did complete 2 lessons though, which means the content wasn't so bad he bounced after 1.

## Top 3 Takeaways
1. **"Lacking direction and soul"** — no clear path (where am I going?), no personality (why should I care?)
2. **Mascot, sound effects, characters are table stakes** — Duolingo comparison means users expect animated personality in learning apps
3. **"No" to return usage** — strongest negative retention signal in the dataset; prototype feel is likely the cause

## Themes

1. **Sign-in is unjustified friction.** Kill the account requirement unless it earns its place.
2. **Onboarding = value demonstration before paywall.** This is the single most important surface. Currently under-delivering.
3. **Social proof is skippable.** Users spam continue and miss the benefit graphs / proof entirely. Add ~2s enforced wait before continue button is tappable.
4. **Onboarding lacks story arc.** Opens cold with a question. Needs a "get started" welcome screen first (Quittr-style), then problem/dream-result question, then graph or review with 2s wait, then "calculating your plan" moment.
